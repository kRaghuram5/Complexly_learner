What Are Complex Numbers?

Have you ever come across equations like x² = -1 and wondered how to solve them? In the world of real numbers, there's no solution. But mathematics doesn’t stop there.

To solve such mysteries, mathematicians introduced a new kind of number — the imaginary unit 'i', where i² = -1.

A complex number is a combination of a real and an imaginary number, written as a + ib. Here, 'a' is the real part, and 'b' is the imaginary part.

Complex numbers are vital in mathematics, physics, and engineering — solving problems in waves, circuits, and quantum mechanics. Curious how they work or want to visualize them? You're in the right place!
